The Estate

A Private Retreat 100 Acres of Living Beauty

Willovita is our private retreat in Winter Springs, Florida. We spent the last several years assembling this property, which includes a number of private guest residences and now totals 100 acres. It was recently certified as a National Wildlife Habitat — a distinction earned through our commitment to sustainably providing essential elements for wildlife to raise and care for their young.

Willovita is home to families of Great Blue Herons, Florida Sandhill Cranes, American Bald Eagles, Ospreys, Hawks, Spotted Owls, Peacocks, Pileated Woodpeckers, and countless other bird varieties — as well as a special selection of horses, Suri alpacas, and several varieties of goats, ducks, and geese.

Behind the Name

Guests would depart renewed and stronger — just like the willow tree.

A Name Rooted in Meaning

Before we selected this name, we were searching for a word to symbolize what our home meant to us, and what we hoped others would experience as they came to visit. The first part of the name — Willo — represents the willow tree. One of the willow's most valuable traits is its flexibility, unusual for trees in being able to bend in outrageous positions without snapping. It also has the ability to thrive in some of the most challenging conditions, when many other trees may not even survive.

We experienced this first-hand in 2017 when our newly planted willows felt the power of Hurricane Irma, bringing 13 inches of rain and wind gusts up to 80 mph. The storm took out some of our great oaks, tall pines, and even sycamore trees. Even though half-submerged in flood waters, our young willow trees came through the storm remarkably stronger than before.

The second half — vita — very simply means "life," and in its true form, happy life. It is our hope that WILLOVITA would be a respite, where people escape from the sounds and clutter of today's culture and discover a place of great conversation and thought, lingering long over memorable dinners. Our goal is to provide a place to rediscover the sounds of nature, the beauty of creation, and the lost arts.
